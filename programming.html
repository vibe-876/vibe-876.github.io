Programming

Programming is very cool. Some of the stuff I program is thrown up onto my Github, although a lot of it just stays on my main machine.

Lisp

I only really use emacs lisp and scheme (guile usually). Emacs lisp has a very obvious use case for me (namely being an emacs user). Scheme on the other hand, I like because of its elegance.

Lisp is just one of those languages where one day everything makes sense, and I love it for that. I also love how simple it is as a concept, as well as how nice a lot of the community is.

Haskell

Haskell is beautiful, there's no doubt in my mind about that. It (much like lisp) appeals to the maths-y side of my mind, and even without that, I love how it manages types.

Lean 4 (still learning)

Lean 4 is a nice counter-part (is that the right word?) to haskell. While haskell has a thing for catagory theory, lean has a thing for type theory (specifically homotopy type theory), which I find really cool.

Lean 4 can also be used for theorem proving, which really appeals to the maths-y side of my brain, and I've been trying to use it to prove some of the things that I'm being taught in the maths half of my degree.

Overall, very cool language.

Rust (still learning)

I've been learning Rust recently -- I've gone through a current chunk of the Rust book, and I'm currently trying to go through advent of code questions in Rust, so that I can reinforce what I've learned. Also, cargo is awesome.

Much like the languages above, I also really like the community of Rust.
